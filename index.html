How to create a clipping mask in Adobe Illustrator

Time Needed: 10 minutes
Level: Beginner Friendly

Are you just beginning to explore the inner workings of Illustrator, but have no idea where to start? Well, then you’ve come to the right place.

In this tutorial, I’ll be taking you step-by-step on how to do one of the simpler yet foundational techniques in Illustrator: creating a clipping mask. Many designers use clipping and layer mask techniques to elevate their images and blend different elements together. Let’s get started!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Adobe Illustrator Access (there is a student subscription)
  • Laptop or iPad
  • An image of your choice

  1. Open Adobe Illustrator.

    On your PC or Mac, find the Adobe Icon with the "Ai" in brown and yellow.

    Opening the Adobe Illustrator Home Screen.

  2. Bring your image into Illustrator.

    To bring an image into Illustrator, the simplest way to do is is to go to your files tab on your PC and drag it to the Illustrator icon on your desktop. (This instruction is for Macbooks) If you have a PC, you can also just enter Illustrator and upload an image by clicking “open.”

    Adobe Illustrator Home Screen, showing how to open a file.

  3. Create a Text Layer.

    This can be anything you want it to be! Just make sure somehow it interacts with where you want your clipping mask to be! Refer to the example, see how Serenity is covering up the silhouette of the man?

    Adding a Text Layer to the background image.

  4. Create a new layer and label it the "Clipping Mask."

    Labeling your layers will help you as a graphic designer to keep track of the elements in your designs, therefore making the process go way more smoothly! Making sure your end result looks like the example below.

    Adding a Clipping Mask Layer to the background image.

  5. Click on the Pen tool to begin tracing your Clipping Mask.

    Outline the part you would like to be the clipping mask with the Pen tool. Make sure that you have the Clipping Mask Layer highlighted when you begin tracing, refer to the example for questions.

    A Pro Tip: Make sure to have Fill turned off so that you can see your progressinstead of a white fill on it as you go.

    Outlining the Clipping Mask.

  6. Complete your clipping mask and make sure it is all connected.

    The beginning of the clipping mask must be connected to the end of your clipping mask to make sure it is one solid object. It should look similar to the example below, no stray pen marks.

    Checking the Clipping Mask for completion and accuracy.

  7. Duplicate your background image.

    This is necessary to create the clipping mask. A simple way to do this is to drag the image later to the symbol with the plus sign in it, or just right-clicking on the layer and selecting "Duplicate Layer."

    For extra resources or tips to create a clipping mask or duplicate a layer, refer to this helpful youtube video.

  8. Drag your duplicate image to the Clipping Mask Layer.

    Make sure your image layer is underneath your clipping mask selection layer. Refer to the example for help.

    Making sure that the Image layer in the Clipping Mask Group is underneath the clipping mask.

  9. Right-Click on your Clipping Mask.

    Make sure your image layer is underneath your clipping mask selection layer is selected as well.

    Pro Tip: I find it helpful to turn off the view on the original layer so I don't accidently click it!

    Making sure that the Image layer in the Clipping Mask Group is underneath the clipping mask is selected.

  10. And Voila! You have created your first Clipping Mask!

    Now you can play around with your text and see where you like it in regards to your clipping mask. Here is a picture of what the layers should look like now.

    This is how the layers should look when completed.
